Day 1 of building Guido

April 2026 · Kavya Shankar

Yesterday was Day 1 of my new project: building a robot to help patients in hospitals. I'm calling it Guido.

The timing isn't a coincidence. I spent the last few weeks in a hospital — as a patient. You notice things from that side that you'd never think about otherwise: how hard it is to find the right department, how often you need something small fetched, how much of a nurse's time goes to navigation and logistics rather than care. I got to be a user before I built the thing. I don't think that was an accident.

Guido is about learning manipulation and navigation in a real, messy, human environment. The first hardware is coming together — an Intel NUC for compute, a 2D LiDAR for mapping, an Intel RealSense for perception, and Kelo wheels for active drive plus passive support. Next up: building the robot's control system.

I'll post hardware updates as it comes together. Follow along on X.
